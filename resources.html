MESSAGES THAT HINDER THE
MENTAL HEALTH
OF MEN

Sometimes the messages that surround us that we see and hear over and over-make it difficult for men to recognize their own mental health and addiction issues and seek professional help. Recognizing and discussing these messages, will help us to change them.

  • Men should be successful and strong → Societal expectations put pressure on men to be strong and successful, where success is measured through power, position and financial security. As a result, self-care and stress management are seen as signs of weakness.
  • It's important for women to take care of themselves→ Self-care and coping skills are important for women and girls; however, this message is not conveyed to men and boys.
  • It's important for women to have work → life balance The balance between career and family is recognized as a concern for women. It is unrecognized as a need for men, even though it is a challenge for men as well.
  • Men's physical symptoms are caused by injury or disease → Men are more likely to describe their mental health concerns as physical symptoms such as headaches or chronic pain. The link between signs of mental illness and physical symptoms is often unrecognized and leads to undiagnosed, untreated men.

HELPLINE NUMBER'S

KIRAN

A 24/7 toll-free helpline that provides support and relief to people with mental illness in 13 languages. The number is 1800-599-0019.

Tele-MANAS

A toll-free helpline that offers multi-language support and connects callers with mental health professionals. The numbers are 14416 or 1-800-891-4416.

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

A 24-hour helpline that provides confidential support to people in emotional distress or suicidal crisis. The number is 988.
